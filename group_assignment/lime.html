Lime

Lime is part of the citrus family and there are several types of lime. Therefore the exact origin of the fruit is uncertain, but it's believed to originate from Indonesia or Southeast Asia. The fruit has been used to flavor food and drinks for countless generations. Since lime has one of the highest concentrations of acids and sugars it’s often the prefered citrus fruit in drinks. For food application the most notable country that uses lime is India and is vital for the dish Onam Sadhya. Lime also packs large amounts of vitamin C the british Navy used Lime to prevent scurvy among its sailors. This was closely guarded military secret back in the days.

Avocado pasta

30 minutes/4 portions

  • 1 lime
  • 1 garlic glove
  • ½ a chili (remove the seeds)
  • 2 ripe avocados
  • salt
  • pepper
  • ½ dl olive-oil
  • a hint of basil
  • 30g (1dl) grated parmesan, pecorino or equivalent cheese
  • 400-500g spaghetti
  1. You can mix everything straight to the bowl where you are planning on serving the dish in.
  2. Add minced garlic and chili to the bowl with the olive-oil and mix. Remove the avocado-seeds, cut them into cubes and add into the mixture. Squeeze in the lime’s juice. Cut the basil and grate the cheese and add them into the bowl with a hint of salt and pepper.
  3. Mix everything well and taste it. The sauce should have enough salt to taste as good as possible
  4. Boil the spaghetti to al dente and add it to the bowl where your sauce already exists
  5. Grate some more cheese on top and a hint of black pepper. Serve immediately
