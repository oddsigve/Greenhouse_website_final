Ginger

Fresh ginger root is a popularly used for seasoning especially in Indian, Chinese, Japanese, Korean and Vietnamese dishes. It is commonly used in stir-fries and curries, marinades and tea as well as baking, as it works particularly well with apple. It's origins are in the tropical rainforests of Southern Asia where it was imported from to Europe through India as a result of spice trade and it was widely used amongst the Romans. Good quality ginger root has a distinctive sharp and strong smell. It can be used in various forms such as ground, pickled and candied.

Ginger chicken

Serves 2 /25 minutes

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 2 tablespoons high-heat oil
  • 200 gram boneless, skinless chicken breast or thigh, cut into bite-size pieces (about Â½ cm thick)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 green onions, ends trimmed, cut crosswise into 2 cm pieces
  • 2 thinly sliced red bell pepper (from about 1 small pepper)
  • 1/2 small onion thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh ginger, cut into matchstick-size pieces
  • Cooked white rice or brown rice, for serving
  1. In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce, and sugar. Keep near the stove, along with the chicken and vegetables.
  2. Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat until very hot.
  3. Add the oil and swirl the pan to coat the bottom.
  4. Add the chicken in an even layer and sear, undisturbed, for about 1 minute.
  5. Add garlic, stir, and continue cooking for 1 minute, stirring constantly. Chicken will be lightly browned, but not cooked through.
  6. Add the green onions, bell pepper, onions, ginger, and sauce. Stir-fry for 2 to 3 minutes, until the chicken is cooked through and the bell peppers and onions are crisp-tender.
  7. Serve immediately, with plain white or brown rice.
