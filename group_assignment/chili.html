Chili

Chili peppers are amongst the oldest plants that people have grown and used as they are great at adding a hint of “heat” in your food. The earliest traces of the usage of chili date back to over 8000 years ago in South America and it was widely used in ancient Aztec-cuisine. Christopher Columbus likely brought chili to Europe from where it spread to Asia with the Portuguese traders. There has also been speculation that the Vikings used chili for medical purposes based on archaeological findings of traces of capsicum frutescens-chili from 13th century found in Sweden.

Chili con carne

4-6 servings / 1 hour

  • 600 gram lean ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 small green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 400 grams cans red kidney beans, rinsed and drained
  • 400 grams cans diced tomatoes
  • 2 to 3 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon pepper
  1. Cook first 4 ingredients in a large skillet over medium-high heat, stirring until beef crumbles and is no longer pink; drain. Place mixture in 5-quart slow cooker; stir in beans and remaining ingredients. Cook at HIGH 3 to 4 hours or at LOW 5 to 6 hours.
  2. Notes: If you want to thicken this saucy chili, stir in finely crushed saltine crackers until the desired thickness is achieved.
