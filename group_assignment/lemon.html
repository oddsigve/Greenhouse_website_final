Lemon

The yellow Lemon fruits origins is unknown, but it’s speculated that it was first grown in northern parts of India, Burma or China. It’s used all over the world in different drink and food recipes. Known drink recipes are Tom Collins and Bloody Mary. It's also widely used in food preparation in countries like India, Mexico, Vietnam, Thailand and in the Peruvian dish Ceviche which has seen an increase in popularity around the world. In parts of Asia and northern Africa they pickle their Lemons to preserve them. These pickled lemons, also known as “country lemon”, are great for storage when resources are limited.

Lemon shrimps

30 mins / 4portions

  • 2 large red bell peppers, diced
  • 4 teaspoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 500g of asparagus, cut into pieces
  • 2 teaspoons freshly grated lemon zest
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • ½ teaspoon salt, divided
  • 5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 400g of shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  1. Add bell peppers, asparagus, lemon zest and 1/4 teaspoon salt to a frying pan with oil and cook, stirring occasionally, until just beginning to soften, about 6 minutes.
  2. Transfer the vegetables to a bowl; cover to keep warm.
  3. Add more oil to the pan with garlic and cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add shrimp and cook, stirring, for 1 minute.
  4. Remove from the heat. Stir in lemon juice, the shrimp, the vegetables and parsley.
