Chives

Chives can be found widely spread in nature in Europe, North America and Asia. It’s commonly grown in home gardens and can be found in many grocery stores. The scapes of chives are used for cooking, but the flowers can also be utilized to for instance, garnish a dish. Usage of chives in European cuisine dates back as far as 5000 years ago, although it cultivated in the Middle Ages. The Romans used chives as a medicine for sore throat and sunburn and in Romanian Gypsy-culture chives have been used for fortune telling and warding off evil and disease.

Chives-rösti potatoes

30 minutes/4 portions

  • 4 big boiled potatoes
  • 2 eggs
  • 0,5dl parmesan-cheese
  • 1 pot of fresh chives
  • 1 chopped onion
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Butter or oil for frying
  1. Peel and grate the potatoes
  2. Roast the chopped onion in oil until golden and crisp
  3. Mix the potatoes with roasted onion, eggs, parmesan, chives with a hint of pepper and salt
  4. Form patties of the mixture and fry them on a pan in medium-heat around 5 minutes per side
