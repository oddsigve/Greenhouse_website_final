Coriander

Coriander or Chinese parsley, as some people refer to it, can be found from the southern parts of Europe to the southwestern parts of Asia.The plant has a fresh green color and all parts of its parts are edible so if you’re feeling adventurous there is room for experimentation. Today coriander is most commonly used in the Indian and Thai cuisines, but is also used in things like barbecue sauce and salad dressing.

Chicken tikka masala

2 hours/4 portions

  • ½ dl plain yoghurt
  • 2 teaspoons of garam masala
  • 2 teaspoons of paprika
  • salt and pepper
  • ½ teaspoon of cayenne pepper
  • ½ teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin
  • 450g boneless chicken breast, sliced into strips
  • 1 large chopped onion
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 fresh green chili peppers
  • 2 large tomatoes, diced
  • 1dl of tomato paste
  • 4 tablespoons of vegetable oil
  • ½ dl water
  • fresh coriander
  1. Combine yogurt and half of the amounts stated of spices in a large bowl. Add the chicken and toss them around the mixture to coat. Cover and let them marinate in the fridge for an hour.
  2. Preheat the oven for 230°C and grease a baking sheet
  3. Place the chicken with space in between the slices on the sheet and bake them in the oven for about 10 minutes. Remove and set aside
  4. Heat the vegetable oil on a large pan in medium heat. Cook and stir the rest of the spices along the tomatoes, tomato paste, garlic, onion and green chillies on there from around 15-20 minutes.
  5. Mix it all with the cooked chicken, cover and let simmer for 15 minutes. Season with ½ teaspoon of salt and garnish with coriander
