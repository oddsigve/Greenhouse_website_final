Oregano

Oregano, with it’s beautiful purple flower and green leafs, is a herb found naturally in the mediterranean and Eurasian regions of the world. It’s also widely used in food preparation in places like Latin America and the Philippines. Oregano is used to lighten up dishes like Kebab and Greek salads, but most famously it has been the herb of the modern Italian cuisine. Fresh, good quality, Oregano should numb your tongue. The popularity of it in America rose after the Second World War after the soldiers overseas developed a taste for the “pizza herb”.

Baked oregano tomatoes

35 minutes/4 portionsl

  • 4 large ripe tomatoes, sliced in 4
  • 0,3dl grated Romano cheese
  • 1dl fresh bread crumbs
  • 1 minced garlic glove
  • ½ teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • salt and pepper
  1. Heat the oven to 200°C and coat a shallow baking dish with cooking spray
  2. Place the slices of tomato in the dish. Sprinkle them with oregano, garlic, cheese, bread crumbs, salt and pepper and drizzle with olive oil
  3. Bake for 20 minutes or until the cheese looks toasted and delicious
