Basil

The name "basil" comes from Greek βασιλικόν φυτόν (basilikón phutón), "royal/kingly plant". There are over 160 named varieties available, with new ones popping up every year. Its origin is, how­ever, not known. It was usually assumed to originate from India, as there some of its close relatives grow wildly. however, as it is attested in the Medi­terranean since 3000 years (Egypt), some doubt may be raised. Newer literature openly speculates whether basil could be a native of West Asia.

Homemade Pesto

5 min/ 5dl

  • 100 gram pine nuts
  • 3 cloves garlic (I think the more, the merrier!)
  • 500 gram fresh basil leaves, packed
  • 100 gram freshly grated Parmesan
  • 1dl extra-virgin olive oil
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste (or about 1/2 tsp. of each)
  1. In a food processor or blender, blend together basil, nuts, garlic, and cheese.
  2. Pour in oil slowly while still mixing, and then add in salt and pepper.
  3. Scrape down the sides and pulse once more to make sure it's well-blended.
